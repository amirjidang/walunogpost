About the Founder – Steve Gadlin

Steve Gadlin, a Chicago native, is the creative mind behind the popular online business “I Want to Draw a Cat For You.” Born on February 27, 1976, in Illinois, Steve’s passion for creative writing led him to study the art at Miami University. He honed his skills as a web developer while working for various companies, but it was his comedic talents that truly set him apart.

As a comedian, Steve entertained audiences with his unique brand of humor, combining wit and charm to captivate his listeners. His talent for making people laugh soon translated into his venture as a businessman, where he found success with “I Want to Draw a Cat For You.”

Combining his creative writing skills, web development expertise, and comedic flair, Steve Gadlin brought the concept of stick figure cat drawings to life. “I Want to Draw a Cat For You” quickly gained popularity, becoming a unique and whimsical way for people to express themselves and own a personalized piece of art.

Driven by his passion for creativity and entrepreneurship, Steve Gadlin has successfully carved a niche for himself in the business world. His ability to turn a simple idea into a profitable venture showcases his ingenuity and determination.

Steve’s journey from a creative writer and web developer to a comedian and successful businessman is a testament to his unwavering dedication and unique approach to business.

Founding the Company

In 2011, Steve Gadlin embarked on a unique venture, launching his online business called “I Want to Draw a Cat For You”. This bold experiment was aimed at testing the viability of a concept that involved selling stick figure drawings of cats to customers. With the initial launch of the website, Steve relied on just a PayPal button and a compelling YouTube video to attract customers. The premise was simple and enticing – customers could have a custom stick figure cat drawing created based on their description for a reasonable price of around $10.

However, despite the simplicity and charm of this idea, the business faced its fair share of challenges. Scalability proved to be one such obstacle, as it was difficult to attract a sufficient number of orders to sustain a thriving enterprise. Yet, undeterred by these hurdles, Steve remained determined to make his venture a success.

Through creative marketing strategies and the compelling appeal of his stick figure cats, Steve sought to overcome these initial challenges and establish a solid foundation for his business. The resulting website launch marked the beginning of a journey that would captivate an audience and create a demand for these whimsical and endearing drawings.

Shark Tank Appearance

In 2012, Steve Gadlin had the opportunity to pitch his unique business idea, “I Want to Draw a Cat For You,” on the popular TV show Shark Tank. Armed with his humor and stick figure cat drawings, Steve aimed to secure a $10,000 investment for 25% of his company.

During his captivating pitch, Steve impressed the panel of Sharks, which included well-known investors such as Mark Cuban, Kevin O’Leary, and Robert Herjavec. Their attention was drawn to the unconventional nature of Steve’s product and its potential in the market.

“I’ve got ten of your cats framed in my office,” said Mark Cuban, expressing his interest in Steve’s cat drawings.

Ultimately, it was Mark Cuban who saw the potential in Steve’s business and made an offer. Mark offered $25,000 for a larger stake of 33% in the company, and Steve gladly accepted the deal. This partnership with Mark Cuban opened up new opportunities for “I Want to Draw a Cat For You” to thrive and expand its reach.

After Shark Tank

After Steve Gadlin appeared on Shark Tank and secured a deal with Mark Cuban, the popularity of “I Want to Draw a Cat For You” soared, resulting in a significant increase in website traffic and orders. Taking advantage of this newfound success, Steve expanded his product line to include cat-themed novelty items, appealing to a broader audience of cat lovers.

Following his appearance on the show, Steve temporarily stepped away from the business to focus on other ventures. He hosted a talk show called Talkin’ Funny, where he interviewed comedians and showcased his own witty humor. Additionally, Steve launched a talent show called Steve Gadlin’s Star Makers, which featured unique performers and entertainers.

In 2021, Steve decided to resurrect “I Want to Draw a Cat For You” and resumed drawing cats for customers. Moreover, Steve made a heartwarming announcement that he would be drawing one cat per day in 2022, with the proceeds going to a non-profit organization dedicated to supporting animal welfare.

It’s evident that even after his success on Shark Tank and his various ventures, Steve Gadlin’s passion for drawing cats remains strong, bringing joy and laughter to cat lovers around the world.

Second Source – The Business’s Journey

After drawing 18,794 cat drawings and receiving a significant investment from Mark Cuban, Steve Gadlin made the difficult decision to temporarily close down his business, “I Want to Draw a Cat For You.” Despite appearing successful on the surface, various factors, such as the investor’s stake and profit margins, influenced this closure.

However, Steve’s passion for his cat drawing career eventually led him to reopen the business and resume delighting customers with his unique stick figure cat drawings.

“I had to take a step back and evaluate the financials and the future prospects of the business. Although the closure was a tough call, it allowed me to recalibrate and think about the long-term sustainability of my cat drawing career,” says Steve Gadlin.

With a newfound perspective, Steve recommitted himself to his creative endeavor, determined to provide joy and laughter to cat enthusiasts around the world.

Through perseverance and the support of his loyal customers, Steve has overcome the challenges that initially led to the business closure. He has now revitalized his cat drawing career and is once again fulfilling orders for his beloved stick figure feline creations.
