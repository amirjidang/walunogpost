Joy Sunday Wiki And Bio

Born to Nigerian parents, Joy Sunday grew up in Staten Island, New York, United States. She joined Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School and completed her theatre training.

The actress later attended USC’s School of Cinematic Arts and graduated with an honors degree in Critical Studies. Joy appeared in several short films before making her television debut in CBS’ 2016 reboot of MacGyver.

She appeared as a top-of-show guest star in the television series. Joy previously played in the short films Joy, Darling, and Embody. She later got her SAG card from Bad Hair (2020), directed and written by Justin Simien.

Bad Hair starred Joy as Cynthia, Zaria Kelley as Young Anna, Corinne Massiah as Young Linda, Elle Lorraine as Anna Bludso, Moses Storm as Male NV6 Exec., Madeleine Byrne as RMV VJ, and James Van Der Beek as Grant Madison.

In 2018, she landed the role of Roa in the television series Yas Kween. Joy starred alongside David Mandell, Casey Thibodaux, Jason DePuy, Chase O’Donnell, Danny Ahrahms, Myles Nuzzi, Peter Vogel, and Tavaris Smith.

The same year she played the role of Stage in the short film “Alive, Again,” directed by Joaquin Dominquez. In 2019, she appeared as Joy in Her Mind in Pieces, Macy in Carol’s Second Act (TV Series), and Janelle in Good Trouble (TV Series).
