What Is Wrong With Erik Johnson Teeth? Pictures Revealed

The American ice hockey player Eric Johnson plays for the Colorado Avalanche of the National Hockey League, and this article gives insight into Johnson’s missing teeth.

Erik Johnson is a 34 years old professional ice hockey player born in Bloomington, Minnesota, the United States, on the 21st of March 1988.

Johnson got picked in the first overall pick by the St. Louis Blues in the 2006 NHL Entry Draft, and he later got traded to the Colorado Avalanche of the National Hockey League.

Colorado Avalanche: What Is Wrong With Erik Johnson Teeth?

Erik Johnson, who serves as the ice hockey defenseman for the Colorado Avalanche of the National Hockey League, has three missing teeth. Three of his front teeth got knocked out in a game in 2011, and he still has kept his fallen tooth safe for his memory.

The athlete also shared the picture of his missing teeth on his social media on the 8th of July 2014; with his tooth in his hand, he captioned it, ‘Look what I found. The original 3’ with an anguished face emoji. Furthermore, Johnson still hasn’t gotten a tooth transplant to date.

Pictures Of Erik Johnson Without His Tooth Revealed

The pictures of Eric Johnson without his tooth are all over his social media as it has already been a decade since he lost his three-front tooth, and he hasn’t chosen to get himself a fake tooth. 

Erik has never felt like hiding his missing tooth while getting photographs. Instead, he always flaunts his captivating smile whenever he gets a chance, and the athlete looks fantastic.

Johnson rarely wears his false teeth as he isn’t fond of wearing them, and he loves doing tricks with his fake teeth whenever he puts them on. Furthermore, the athlete won the best hockey smile beating other ice hockey players despite having missing teeth. 

How Erik Johnson Lost His Tooth? Explore The Reason Behind His Missing Tooth

Ice hockey players often lose some of their teeth when they get hit in the mouth with a puck or stick during a game. And if in case such incidents happen, they receive treatments from their team dentist.

And Erik Johnson, an ice hockey player, lost his tooth for the same reason; he lost them while in a practice match and screened the goalie. His teammates shot the puck that hit him in the mouth, but he shared that the cut on his lip was more painful than losing his teeth. Furthermore, the athlete was still with the St. Louis Blues and got traded to Colorado Avalanche right after the accident happened.
