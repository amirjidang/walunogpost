Early Life and Career

Jadakiss, born as Jason Terrance Phillips, hails from Yonkers, New York, where he developed a passion for hip-hop at a young age. His journey in the music industry began on the streets, where he honed his skills through freestyle rapping. It was during this time that he crossed paths with Styles P and Sheek Louch, two fellow talented artists who would later become his close friends and collaborators.

At the age of 12, Jadakiss caught the attention of Ruff Ryders Entertainment after participating in a freestyle competition in Florida. Recognizing his raw talent, he formed a rap group called The Warlocks alongside Styles P and Sheek Louch, which eventually evolved into The Lox.

The Lox gained prominence through their collaborations with music heavyweights such as Mary J. Blige, Puff Daddy, and Mariah Carey. Their unique style and lyrical prowess resonated with audiences and propelled them to success. Jadakiss’s solo career took off with the release of his debut album, “Kiss Tha Game Goodbye,” in 2001, which set the stage for a series of successful albums that solidified his position in the industry.
