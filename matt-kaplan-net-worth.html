Matthew Kaplan, also known as Matt Kaplan, is an American financier and film producer. He is well-known for producing films for young adults, such as the To All the Boys film series. He is the founder and chief executive officer of Ace Entertainment, as well as the former president of Awesomeness Films.

How Did Matt Kaplan Grow Up?

Matt Kaplan was born on April 14, 1984, in Los Angeles, California, United States. Kaplan received his bachelor’s degree from Columbia University, where he played quarterback for the Columbia Lions football team. After graduating from college, CBS President and CEO Les Moonves employed him as an intern before promoting him to director of digital development.

Then, he joined Lionsgate Films, where he rose to the position of SVP of Development and Production, supervising films starring Amy Poehler and Paul Rudd, such as They Came Together.

Kaplan founded Chapter One Films in 2013 and signed a first-look agreement with Blumhouse Productions, producing films such as 6 Miranda Drive and The Lazarus Effect. In 2015, Jeffrey Katzenberg appointed him to lead the film division of Awesomeness. In 2017, he established Ace Entertainment, which produced To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before and its sequels.
