What has Jon Pointing been in?

Jon Pointing, born in 1986 or 1987, has evolved from creating online comedy sketches to becoming a notable English actor, writer, and comedian.

His journey to fame started on his YouTube channel, “JonPointingComedy,” where he gained recognition for witty and relatable sketches.

This success paved the way for his role in Channel 4’s sketch show “Pls Like,” showcasing his comedic timing and writing skills.

The pivotal point in Pointing’s career came with his starring role in the historical comedy series “Plebs,” where he portrayed the lovable yet dim-witted slave, Aurelius.

The show, spanning four seasons, solidified his fanbase with his humorous take on ancient Roman life.

In 2023, Pointing soared to new heights with his role in the dark comedy series “Big Boys.”

His nuanced portrayal of a struggling musician dealing with addiction earned him critical acclaim and a nomination for the prestigious British Academy Television Award for Best Male Comedy Performance.

Beyond acting, Pointing is a skilled writer, co-authoring the sitcom “Stath Lets Flats” and contributing to various comedy shows. His sharp wit and observational humor shine through in both his writing and acting.

Aged 36-37, Jon Pointing is a rising star in British comedy, lauded for his versatility, comedic prowess, and ability to tackle intricate roles.

With an award nomination and ongoing projects, Pointing’s career promises to keep audiences entertained for years to come.
