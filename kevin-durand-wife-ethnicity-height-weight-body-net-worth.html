Brief Bio (Ethnicity)

Kevin was born on January 14, 1974 in Thunder Bay, Ontario to Canadian parents Reina and Serge Durand. He is of French Canadian descent. He attended St. Ignatius High School in Thunder Bay.

Growing up, Durand, like many children, wanted to bemany things. He played hockey so his family thought he could just take it professionally but no, Kevin wanted to be something else, a rapper. He soon found that he had a penchant for comedy, and not just any other form of comedy, but stand-up comedy. He immediately abandoned his rapper ambitions and embraced his newfound passion and love for stand-up comedy, earning the title as one of ” Canada’s Funniest New Comedians ” in 1994.

That same year, he moved to Hollywood to continue pursuing his dreams in the entertainment industry. He made his acting debut in the 1997 television series Exhibit A: Secrets of Forensic Science. His first big break came in 1999 when he starred in the comedy-drama as “Tree” Lane. Mystery, Alaska.

Due to his distinctive facial features, suave Kevin Durand often gets the role of a police officer, a brutal thug or a gangster. For example, we saw him play Walking Tall in the 2004 action film Booth, Hamilton’s (Neal McDonough) right-hand man, which also starred Dwayne Johnson (The Rock).

He portrayed sidekick Red in Wild Pigs, as Fred J. Dukes in X-Men Origins: Wolverine, and as Jeeves Tremor (Psychotic Neo-Nazi) in the 2007 British-American action thriller Smokin’ Aces.

He played Gabriel in Scott Stewart’s 2010 apocalyptic supernatural action-horror film Legion, as Little John in the epic war film Robin Hood (2010), and as hired thug Tucker in the remake of 3:10 to Yuma. In 2012, he played Lenny Jackson in Edwin Boyd: Gangsters, which earned him a Genie Awards nomination for Best Supporting Actor. That same year he played Torval in David Cronenberg’s drama-thriller Cosmopolis.

He also starred in The Truth and as a video game character in Resident Evil: Vengeance His other films and TV series include T ie Mortal Instruments, Noah, The Captive, Devil’s Knot, The Strain, Vikings, Dark Angel, Touching Evil, and more. He has also made guest appearances on Dead Like Me, Without a Trace, CSI: Miami, CSI, ER, and others.

Kevin Durand has also starred in a number of Off-Broadway stage productions including The Adventures of Tom Sawyer where he played the role of Injun Joe. He hasn’t entirely given up his love of singing, once appearing as a guest vocalist with Russell Crowe’s former band 30 Odd Foot of Grunts.
