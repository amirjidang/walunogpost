Twitter Slams Steve Harvey For Suggesting Men Cant Be Friends With Women

A video of American presenter and actor Steve Harvey where he suggests men can’t be friends with women has gone viral.

In a 2010 interview, Harvey explains he doesn’t have any female friends, partly due to his wife but mostly to his incapability of establishing meaningful, Platonic relationships with women.

“I’m incapable of that,” Harvey says in the video, reposted on TikTok.

Steve Harvey Suggests Men And Women Can’t Be Friends

When the reporter asks him why is that, he seems hesitant. Then he proceeds to inform the offscreen reporter that she is “an attractive woman” as somehow this is relevant to what he is about to say.

“There is some guy, somewhere, saying, ‘Yeah, we’re friends.’ No, that’s not true,” Harvey says.

“He’s your friend only because you have made it absolutely clear that nothing else is happening except this friendship [you] have,” he continues.

The actor and presenter seems to suggest that all men are unable to strike a friendship with a woman because they are inevitably waiting for something romantic and/or sexual to happen.

"We remain your friends in hope that one day there’ll be a crack in the door, a chink in the armor and trust and believe, that guy that you think is just your buddy, he will slide in that crack the moment he gets the opportunity,” Harvey says.

“Cause we’re guys,” he adds.

He finally tells the reporter: “99.9% of us think that way.”

Steve Harvey Slammed On Socials For Sexist Statements

While some took to social media to defend Harvey and his decade-old interview, many have been slamming him for his sexist views.

“I’m sayin tho if you’re building a friendship with a woman only based on the fact one day you might be able to take advantage of her trust then there’s some issues you have to workout for yourself,” one user commented.

“Steve Harvey basically said, “I objectify women, but who doesn’t? It is what it is,” was another comment.

"Steve Harvey is a misogynist and sexist so let’s start there. He is ‘incapable’ of being friends with woman because he doesn’t see their value as people beyond what he can get for them sexually. That’s a flaw on his part. If he viewed women as people, this wouldn’t be a problem,” another tweet reads.

"steveharvey do be sounding like half the guys I went to high school with," one user also tweeted.
