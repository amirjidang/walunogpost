10 Celebrities Who Refuse To Do Superhero Movies (&amp; Why)

Since the success of The Dark Knight trilogy and the MCU, big studios have been producing superhero movies and TV shows to please the large audience. It's a great time for comic book fans to be alive because they can watch their favorite superheroes be brought to life, sometimes even by their favorite actor.

RELATED: Ashton Kutcher Was Almost Cast As Clark Kent In 'Superman Returns'

The superhero genre has gained so much popularity that a majority of big-time actors have expressed interest in appearing in superhero movies. However, not every actor in Hollywood has been bitten by the superhero bug. Let's look at some celebrities that have turned down superhero roles and why they refuse to be in superhero movies.

10 Jodie Foster

Jodie Foster is one of Hollywood's most successful celebrities, appearing in classics, like Silence of the Lambs, Taxi Driver, and Maverick. She has a wide range, appearing in thrillers, action flicks, and even comedies. She has even taken on directing, showing off her expertise directing movies like The Beaver and Money Monster.

One thing she will absolutely not do is ever star in or direct a superhero movie, claiming "It’s ruining the viewing habits of the American population and then ultimately the rest of the world.” She's not a fan of the trend and will most likely never appear in any MCU or DCEU movie.

9 Jason Statham

Jason Statham is one of the biggest action stars in Hollywood, being a part of big franchises and known as an overall badass. It would be amazing to see him take on a superhero role or maybe even a villain role in a superhero movie, but unfortunately, that will most likely never happen.

RELATED: 10 Hidden Gem Movies On Netflix Right Now

When asked about him ever being in a superhero movie, he didn't mince words and said "Anybody can do it", saying that all the green screens and stunt doubles take away from the authentic experience.

8 Rose Mcgowan

Rose Mcgowan has been in a handful of movies throughout the years, just not superhero ones. Mcgowan is most well-known for her role in Scream and Charmed and has decided to stick to thrillers or slasher films.

In an interview, she was highly critical of the genre, claiming that it was played out heavily "I don't want green goblins and tight outfits. I want intelligence, daring, work that drives society forward. I want a mirror, not every cliche regurgitated."

7 Mel Gibson

Mel Gibson has had a love-hate relationship with Hollywood, being a talented actor/director, but also being the subject to plenty of scandals. He has made some of the greatest movies to date and is well known for his realistic practical effects and his extreme method acting.

So it's no surprise that the old thespian would be against superhero movies, claiming that studios waste money on these big movies and they still tank. He also claims that the repetitive reboots like Spider-man or Batman are ruining the experience.

6 Josh Hartnett

A name a lot of people haven't heard in a long time is Josh Hartnett. The 42-year-old actor has retired from acting, claiming that it was only part of his journey and he always had plans to do other things besides being in front of a camera. As a handsome, young, and tall actor, he has been offered many superhero roles throughout the years, including Superman, Batman, and even Spider-man.

RELATED: Spider-Man: Far From Home: Marisa Tomei Is Fed Up Of Playing The Role Of "The Mom"

He turned down the roles because he did not want to get defined as a superhero for the rest of his career, he wanted to make his own name for himself.

5 Jon Hamm

Making his claim to fame in the hit show Mad Men,  Jon Hamm has been connected to several superhero roles throughout the years and even heavily considered to play Batman on several occasions.

In 2011, he was even asked to play the Green Lantern before the role went to Ryan Reynolds, claiming it was something that he didn't want to do. He went into further explaining his refusal to do superhero movies by saying there is too much risk in signing up and having to do multiple movie deals on scripts that haven't been written or without knowing the success of the movies.

4 Kate Beckinsale

Kate Beckinsale is best known for her work in the Underworld franchise and is one of the most sought out female action stars. Due to her roles, she was even approached to play the mightiest female hero of them all in Wonder Woman but turned the role because she wasn't a fan of the script or the costume.

Even though she didn't necessarily say she wouldn't make a superhero movie, she did say she's concerned wearing the costumes would one day embarrass her kids.

3 Amanda Seyfried

Amanda Seyfried has been in several comedies and romantic movies, often playing the love interest or the unlikely hero. As a talented actress, she was offered the role of Gamora in the Guardians of the Galaxy but turned it down due to the commitment she would've had to make for the role.

RELATED: American Horror Story Season 10: What We Know So Far

In an interview promoting one of her latest movies, she was asked about turning down a superhero role and she said: "Part of me wishes I had done it, but the other part of me is like, 'I had a life to live,' and I don't think I would've been happy."

2 Jennifer Aniston

Jennifer Anniston is one of the biggest stars in Hollywood today, appearing in hundreds of movies and tv shows, across all genres. One genre we have never seen her in, and will probably never see her in is the superhero genre.

Like many of the actors on this list, she doesn't think the movies are authentic saying “You’re seeing what’s available out there and it’s just diminishing and diminishing in terms of, it’s big Marvel movies or things that I’m not just asked to do or really that interested in living in a green screen.” It is a shame because she definitely has the chops to play big-name characters, we will just never see her take them on.

1 Martin Scorsese

Arguably one of the greatest directors of our time and a cinematic genius, Martin Scorsese has made some of the best movies of all time. With his genius and expert storytelling, we'd love to see him direct the next big superhero film but that will most likely never happen.

Scorsese has, through the years, dissed the MCU and DCEU, comparing the experience to theme parks. Although he's not against successful movies like Black Panther Marvel movies take away from his vision, and he has said that.
