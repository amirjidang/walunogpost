Early Life and Childhood

Unfortunately, Kristy’s parents divorced when she was just three years old. So, along with her brothers, Kristy went to live with their mother. After the divorce, her mother, Carolyn, remarried and gave birth to Jennifer Lucas’s daughter. Kristy was actively involved in acting and modelling from a young age, and she dreamed of becoming a successful actress one day.

Professional Life

When she was just 11 years old, Kristy made her first appearance on television by playing the small role of ‘Steffi’ in an episode of the series ‘Love, American Style’. In the same year, she portrayed the supporting role of Patricia Apple in the series ‘Apple’s Way’. After this, she appeared as a guest in series like ‘ABC Afterschool Special,’ ‘Sara,’ and ‘The Bionic Woman.’ Kristy’s life turned upside down when she got a chance to play Letitia “Buddy” Lawrence in the family drama ‘Family’ in 1976. Kristy won two ‘Emmy Awards’ for ‘Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Drama Series for her outstanding performance in the series.’

She also got a ‘Golden Globe nomination for her excellent performance. In 1980, she portrayed the first lead role of her career in the film ‘Little Darlings.’ Apart from this, Kristy played in several other movies and TV shows such as ‘Only When I Laugh’, ‘White Dog’, ‘The Pirate Movie’, ‘Just the Way You Are’, ‘Dream Lover’, ‘Two Moon Junction’, ‘Baby of the Bride’, ‘Empty Nest’, and ‘Invasion America. Furthermore, she even voiced ‘The Princess in the 1982 film ‘Aladdin and the Magic Lamp.’ Kristy also co-produced the 1993 movie ‘Mother of the Bride’ and was an assistant producer for the 1989 short film ‘Dirty Tennis.’ In addition to this, she even contributed to the soundtracks of ‘Only When I Laugh,’ ‘The Pirate Movie,’ and ‘The Night the Lights Went Out in Georgia.’
