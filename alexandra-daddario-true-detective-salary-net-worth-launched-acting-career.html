Alexandra Daddario May Not Have Been Paid Well On True Detective But The Show Changed Her Net Worth

Alexandra Daddario is renowned for her remarkable acting skills and mesmerizing blue eyes. With leading roles in movies such as the Percy Jackson franchise, Baywatch, and Texas Chainsaw 3D, as well as glossy roles in HBO's The White Lotus and Mayfair Witches, Daddario is proving herself to be a force to be reckoned with.

However, at the start of her career, Daddario was eager to demonstrate the full extent of her abilities. Her opportunity to do so came with her role in the first season of HBO's True Detective back in 2014, where she played Lisa Tragnetti.

Although she was given only a small role on the show, it opened even more doors for her and increased her visibility in Hollywood. Daddario's salary in True Detective was not disclosed, but her role enabled her career trajectory to continue on an upward status, propelling her to even greater heights.

How Much Did Alexandra Daddario Get Paid For True Detective?

Alexandra Daddario has had an illustrious career in Hollywood, with her role in the Percy Jackson films widely credited for catapulting her into stardom. However, Daddario has revealed that her much smaller role in the hit HBO series True Detective kick-started her career as an actress.

Although her salary for the show was unknown, a discussion on Quora revealed that, in the realm of HBO shows, supporting actors typically earn anywhere between $50,000 and $150,000 per episode. However, this range can vary significantly based on several key factors like the actor's level of experience, the particular show they are involved in, the nature of their role, and their negotiation abilities during contract discussions.

In a 2020 interview with The Hollywood Reporter, Daddario explained that before landing the role in True Detective, she was struggling to be taken seriously as an actor. Frustrated with her career's stagnation, she made a tactical decision to fight for a role in True Detective.

Daddario elaborated on her strategy, saying, "I remember really fighting for that role or any role in the series because I was at a blockade at that time in my career. I remember I was not getting into audition rooms for roles I really thought I was right for, and I was struggling to be taken seriously like every actor has."

Daddario played the character of Lisa Tragnetti, a court reporter who becomes involved with Woody Harrelson's character, in the first season of True Detective. She had auditioned for a different role in the show, but she strategically pushed to be cast in any role to gain exposure in the industry.

Her decision to join the show paid off when an episode featuring a daring scene with her and Harrelson aired. Daddario's manager informed her the next morning that her phone had been ringing off the hook with offers from various producers and directors.

Reflecting on her experience, Daddario called her decision to join True Detective a "tactical decision," aimed at showcasing her range as an actress and gaining exposure in the industry. The show was a game-changer for her career and allowed her to secure more prominent roles.

How Did Alexandra Daddario Feel About Not Being Invited To The Premiere Of True Detective?

In an interview with Collider, Alexandra Daddario reflected on the unpredictability of her career, saying that her biggest successes often came unexpectedly. Looking back, Daddario expressed surprise that her character on True Detective resonated so deeply with audiences, even though she didn't foresee it at the time.

Despite her strong performance in True Detective, Daddario revealed that she wasn't invited to the show's premiere and didn't anticipate the massive impact the show would have on her career.

"I mean, everyone has an ego, but I’m like, 'If they want me, they'll call me,' you know? And if not, I need to build a rich enough life apart from that to be happy. I didn't expect to be invited. It was only afterward in retrospect when I was at the Emmys that I was like, 'Wow, I’m a big enough part of this show to be invited to the Emmys on behalf of the show even though I’m not nominated.' But it was just something that I don't think anybody expected including myself. I really didn't expect people to have that reaction to that character the way that they did."

What Other Projects Has Alexandra Daddario Starred In After True Detective?

Alexandra Daddario has an extensive list of film and television credits post-True Detective. In 2014, she appeared in the zombie comedy Burying the Ex, followed by a leading role in the disaster film San Andreas in 2015, alongside Dwayne Johnson. She also starred in the Nicholas Sparks romantic drama The Choice, a 2017 Baywatch film with Zac Efron, the comedy The Layover with Kate Upton, the psychological thriller Night Hunter, and the mystery film We Have Always Lived in the Castle.

In 2019, she produced and starred in the slasher film We Summon the Darkness and executive produced and starred in Can You Keep a Secret?, a romantic comedy based on the book of the same name. In 2020, she appeared in the dystopian film Songbird, which was the first film shot in Los Angeles during the COVID pandemic.

On the television front, Daddario had a three-episode arc on the anthology series American Horror Story: Hotel in 2015, playing the character of famous costume designer Natacha Rambova.

She has also appeared in the comedy series The Last Man on Earth, the animated sketch series Robot Chicken, and the sitcom Workaholics. In 2019, she was part of the ensemble cast of the anthology streaming series Why Women Kill, along with Lucy Liu and Ginnifer Goodwin. Later in 2021, Daddario had a recurring role on the Starz network's The Girlfriend Experience and also had multi-episode runs on shows like White Lotus and Mayfair Witches.
