The 10 Highest-Grossing Dania Ramirez Movies, Ranked

Quick Links

  • She Hate Me - 2004 ($1.4 Million)
  • Illegal Tender - 2007 ($3 Million)
  • 25th Hour - 2002 ($25.3 Million)

  • Quarantine - 2008 ($41 Million)

  • X-Men: The Last Stand - 2006 ($459 Million)

Movie buffs and couch potatoes, allow us to introduce you to a list that will make your eyes pop. We're talking about the top 10 highest-grossing Dania Ramirez movies, ranked and ready for your perusal. Whether you're a fan of her sassy roles or her sultry charm, you surely cannot ignore the strong presence of Dania Ramirez. From action-packed blockbusters to heartwarming dramas, she's given us a taste of her versatile talent and left us wanting more. Enough chitchat, let's get ready to be entertained. Without further ado, let's roll out the red carpet for the top 10 highest-grossing Dania Ramirez movies.

Mojave - 2016 ($9,213)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Did you catch Ramirez in the indie thriller Mojave? Playing Detective Beaumont, she investigates a deadly encounter in the desert. Although the movie only grossed $9,213 worldwide, Ramirez's performance was the real treasure here, critics praised her for bringing both intensity and depth to the role. Looks like this diamond in the rough was worth a watch after all!

She Hate Me - 2004 ($1.4 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

Remember the satirical comedy She Hate Me? Ramirez played Alex Guerrero, a woman who offers her ex-boyfriend the chance to be a sperm donor for a fee. The movie didn't exactly impregnate the box office, grossing just $1.4 million worldwide, but Ramirez's performance was a definite highlight. Critics showered her with praise, proving that sometimes it's the smaller roles that pack the biggest punch.

Illegal Tender - 2007 ($3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the action-packed Illegal Tender, Dania Ramirez plays Ana, the daughter of a notorious drug lord, who's forced to flee for her life with her family. While the movie may not have exactly taken the box office by storm, Ramirez's performance was a breath of fresh air. Critics couldn't get enough of her standout role, proving that sometimes the most badass performances come in the smallest packages. Having earned $3,054,430 in worldwide gross, this movie was definitely worth its weight in gold.

25th Hour - 2002 ($25.3 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In 25th Hour, Dania Ramirez played Daphne, the girlfriend of the main character's best friend, she brings an extra layer of emotion to an already poignant film. Though her role was brief, Ramirez's performance is anything but forgettable. Critics praised her for stealing every scene she's in. With a $25,344,490 worldwide box office gross, this movie was definitely worth every tear shed.

Premium Rush - 2012 ($31.3 Million)

Via Flickr/Catchplay

Who says women can't handle the heat in action-packed movies? Ramirez proves them wrong as Vanessa, a bike messenger caught up in a dangerous game of cat and mouse in Premium Rush. Her performance showcases her acting range as she navigates the high-stakes world of bike messengers in New York City. With a worldwide gross of $31,393,201, this movie is a testament to Ramirez's ability to hold her own in a male-dominated genre.

Quarantine - 2008 ($41 Million)

Via Flickr/Grave Reviews

Ramirez plays Sadie, one of the unfortunate residents trapped in a sealed-off apartment building. With her convincing performance, she'll have you on the edge of your seat, and checking your temperature for days to come. The movie may have grossed over $41 million worldwide, but the real scare comes from Ramirez's bone-chilling performance.

Fat Albert - 2004 ($48 Million)

Via Flickr/Plex

In this wholesome family movie, Ramirez takes on the role of Lauri, a kind-hearted friend who helps the main character navigate the ups and downs of teenage life. While the movie may not have been a box office sensation, Ramirez's warm and engaging performance was a definite highlight that made audiences feel like they were part of the gang.

American Reunion - 2012 ($236 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

As Selena in the much-anticipated fourth installment of the American Pie series, Ramirez's performance brings plenty of laughs and romance to the big screen. With a mix of outrageous humor and touching moments, this movie was a reunion we all couldn't wait to RSVP to. With a whopping $236 million in box office sales, it's clear that fans couldn't get enough of the gang's antics.

X-Men: The Last Stand - 2006 ($459 Million)

Via Wikimedia Commons

In the epic finale of the original X-Men trilogy, Ramirez portrays Callisto, a fierce mutant with extraordinary powers. Her electrifying presence adds a unique flavor to the star-studded cast, even though her screen time may be limited. Nonetheless, Ramirez's magnetic performance leaves a lasting impression that electrifies the film's epic action sequences, contributing to its box office success, as it grossed over $459 Million worldwide.

Jumanji: The Next Level - 2019 ($798 Million)

Via Flickr/TV Insider

In the thrilling sequel to the popular Jumanji franchise, Ramirez takes on the role of the Lady in Red, a mysterious and powerful character who adds a spark of excitement to the already action-packed adventure. Despite her limited screen time, Ramirez delivers a memorable performance that leaves a lasting impression. With a box office gross of nearly $800 million, it's safe to say that Jumanji: The Next Level was a major success that exceeded everyone's expectations.
